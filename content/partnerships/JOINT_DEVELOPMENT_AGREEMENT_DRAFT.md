# Joint Development Agreement

**Template v.2 — Dual-Route, Template-Neutral**

---

THIS JOINT DEVELOPMENT AGREEMENT ("Agreement") is made on the Effective Date specified in Annexure A.

**BETWEEN:**

(1) The Launch Entity, as described in Annexure A; and

(2) The Development Promoter, as described in Annexure A.

**WHEREAS:**

A. The Launch Entity is undertaking the planning and phased development of a BioHub ecosystem (which may include or be designated as a Smart City, Special Economic Zone, or equivalent integrated development, as specified in Annexure A) and requires the identification and delivery of essential infrastructure platforms necessary for long-term sustainability and operational resilience.

B. The Originating Party (as specified in Annexure A) has identified the strategic need for the infrastructure platform described in Annexure A (the "Project") to support the objectives and operational requirements set out in Annexure A (Project Objectives).

C. Where the Origination Route (as defined below) is Route A, the Launch Entity or BioHub ecosystem identified the demand for the Project and sourced the Development Promoter to lead its development. Where the Origination Route is Route B, the Development Promoter independently identified the Project opportunity and approached the Launch Entity or NexusNovus for structuring, incubation, and financing support.

D. The Development Promoter possesses expertise in infrastructure origination, structuring, consortium formation, and institutional financing preparation.

E. The Parties wish to collaborate during the development phase of the Project for the purposes of structuring, preparing, and progressing the Project toward bankability and Financial Close.

F. The Parties acknowledge that this Agreement governs the development phase only and does not constitute a partnership, joint venture, or operational management agreement beyond Financial Close.

**NOW, THEREFORE, the Parties agree as follows:**

---

## Clause 1 — Purpose and Background

1.1 The Parties acknowledge that the BioHub ecosystem (as specified in Annexure A) requires the identification, origination, and delivery of essential infrastructure platforms necessary for the proper functioning, resilience, and long-term sustainability of that ecosystem.

1.2 The Originating Party (as specified in Annexure A) has identified the strategic need for the Project to support the operational requirements set out in Annexure A (Project Objectives).

1.3 The Parties further acknowledge that the Project is at an early origination stage and requires structured development, consortium formation, technical definition, and financing preparation prior to implementation or operational deployment.

1.4 The purpose of this Joint Development Agreement (the "Agreement") is to appoint the Development Promoter for the Project, to define the scope of its development mandate, and to set out the economic principles governing such role during the development phase only.

1.5 This Agreement is expressly limited to the development, structuring, and financing preparation of the Project and does not govern long-term operations, management, or ownership arrangements beyond the achievement of Financial Close, which shall be addressed under separate agreements, if applicable.

---

## Clause 2 — Parties

2.1 This Agreement is entered into between:

(a) The Launch Entity, being the entity identified in Annexure A (Launch Entity Name) and duly incorporated under the laws of the jurisdiction specified in Annexure A (Governing Law), acting in its capacity as BioHub launch and development entity (the "Launch Entity"); and

(b) The Development Promoter, being the entity identified in Annexure A (Development Promoter Name) and duly incorporated under the laws of the jurisdiction specified in Annexure A (Governing Law) (the "Development Promoter").

2.2 The Parties acknowledge that:

(a) where a Landholding Entity is specified in Annexure A, such entity serves the role described in Annexure A (Landholding Entity Role) and is not a party to this Agreement unless expressly stated; and

(b) NexusNovus acts as an independent structuring and de-risking platform and may be engaged in connection with the Project, but is not a contracting party to this Agreement unless expressly agreed in writing.

2.3 Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating any partnership, joint venture, employment relationship, or agency relationship between the Parties beyond the limited development collaboration expressly set out herein.

---

## Clause 3 — Definitions and Interpretation

3.1 In this Agreement, unless the context otherwise requires, the following terms shall have the meanings set out below:

- **BioHub Ecosystem**: an integrated development ecosystem comprising multiple interdependent infrastructure platforms, utilities, and ventures sharing common demand logic, as further identified in Annexure A (Ecosystem Name, Ecosystem Type).

- **Catalytic Funding**: non-dilutive or concessional funding sources, including grants, technical assistance, feasibility funding, innovation subsidies, or similar instruments intended to finance early-stage development activities.

- **Development Period**: the period commencing on the Effective Date and ending upon Financial Close or termination in accordance with this Agreement.

- **Financial Close**: execution of binding financing documentation resulting in committed project funding, including but not limited to DFI financing, ECA-backed facilities, supplier credit, buyer credit, PPP structures, sovereign guarantee-backed structures, or equivalent contract funding structures enabling implementation of not less than the percentage of Project capital expenditure specified in Annexure A (Financial Close Threshold).

- **Incubation Framework**: the project structuring, standardisation, and de-risking process operated by NexusNovus for the purpose of preparing projects for institutional and development finance eligibility, including feasibility assessment, capital structure design, risk separation, consortium formation support, and financing preparation.

- **Originating Party**: the Party that identified the strategic need for the Project, as specified in Annexure A.

- **Origination Route**: the classification of the Project's development origin, being either:
  - (a) **Route A (Venture Foundry)** — where the project need was identified by the Launch Entity or BioHub ecosystem, and the Development Promoter was subsequently sourced and appointed; or
  - (b) **Route B (Promoter-led)** — where the Development Promoter independently identified the project opportunity and approached the Launch Entity or NexusNovus for structuring, incubation, and financing support.
  - The applicable Origination Route is specified in Annexure A.

- **Project**: the proposed infrastructure platform to be developed within or for the BioHub Ecosystem, as further described in Clause 4 and Annexure A (Project Description).

3.2 References to statutes or regulations shall include any amendment or replacement thereof.

3.3 Headings are for convenience only and shall not affect interpretation.

---

## Clause 4 — Project Description

4.1 The Project comprises the origination, structuring, and development of the infrastructure platform described in Annexure A (Project Description), intended to operate as an essential utility within the BioHub Ecosystem (the "Project Platform").

4.2 The Project Platform is intended to support the operational functions and capabilities set out in Annexure A (Project Platform Functions).

4.3 At the date of this Agreement, the Project remains in an early development and origination phase and has not reached final technical design, capital structure determination, or operational configuration.

4.4 The Parties acknowledge that the precise scope, scale, and technical configuration of the Project Platform may evolve during the Development Period as a result of feasibility studies, engineering work, consortium formation, regulatory engagement, and financing requirements.

4.5 The specific Project name, description, territory, and ecosystem context are set out in Annexure A (Project Variables).

---

## Clause 5 — Development Framework and Pathway

5.1 The Parties agree that the Project shall be developed through a structured development pathway designed to progress the Project from origination to bankability and financing readiness.

5.2 The development pathway shall include, where appropriate:

(a) conceptual refinement and platform definition;

(b) technical and engineering coordination;

(c) consortium identification and formation;

(d) preparation of feasibility, compliance, and ESG documentation;

(e) engagement with potential offtakers, vendors, and institutional stakeholders;

(f) preparation for institutional and development finance eligibility.

5.3 The Parties acknowledge that the development pathway is inherently iterative and may require adjustment in response to regulatory, technical, financial, or market conditions.

5.4 No Party guarantees that the Project will reach Financial Close, and all development activities are undertaken on a best-efforts basis subject to funding availability and external approvals.

---

## Clause 6 — NexusNovus Incubation Framework

6.1 The Parties acknowledge that the Project is intended to be processed through the NexusNovus Incubation Framework for the purpose of transforming the Project into a format suitable for institutional and development finance consideration.

6.2 The Incubation Framework process is intended to:

(a) standardise the Project structure;

(b) separate development, credit, and market risks;

(c) define appropriate capital structure and security principles;

(d) prepare the Project for Frontier Funding eligibility.

6.3 The Development Promoter shall cooperate in good faith with NexusNovus in connection with all reasonable structuring, documentation, and information requirements necessary to progress the Project through the Incubation Framework.

6.4 The Parties acknowledge that NexusNovus acts independently and that its participation does not constitute a guarantee of financing, underwriting, or investment commitment.

---

## Clause 7 — Role and Mandate of the Development Promoter

7.1 The Launch Entity hereby appoints the Development Promoter as development promoter of the Project for the duration of the Development Period, and the Development Promoter accepts such appointment on a best-efforts basis.

7.2 The Development Promoter's mandate is expressly limited to the development phase of the Project and shall include responsibility for:

(a) leading project origination and development activities;

(b) coordinating technical, commercial, and consortium inputs required for project definition;

(c) progressing the Project through the NexusNovus Incubation Framework;

(d) supporting engagement with Development Finance Institutions, Export Credit Agencies, catalytic funders, and other financing stakeholders;

(e) preparing the Project for Frontier Funding eligibility and Financial Close.

7.3 The Parties expressly acknowledge that the Development Promoter is not appointed to manage, operate, or run the Project Platform following Financial Close, and that long-term operational management shall be determined separately.

7.4 Nothing in this Agreement obliges the Development Promoter to assume any executive, fiduciary, or operational role in the Project beyond the Development Period, although such role may be agreed by the Parties in separate agreements following Financial Close.

---

## Clause 8 — Development Period

8.1 The Development Period shall commence on the Effective Date of this Agreement and shall continue until the earliest of:

(a) achievement of Financial Close;

(b) termination of this Agreement in accordance with Clause 19; or

(c) written agreement of the Parties to conclude the Development Period.

8.2 The Parties may, by mutual written agreement, extend the Development Period where:

(a) the Project is demonstrably progressing toward Financial Close; and

(b) continued development activities are commercially and strategically justified.

8.3 During the Development Period, the Development Promoter shall act in good faith and use reasonable endeavours to advance the Project in accordance with the development pathway described in this Agreement.

---

## Clause 9 — Economic Entitlements of the Development Promoter

9.1 In consideration for the Development Promoter's role and activities during the Development Period, the Development Promoter shall be entitled to the economic consideration set out in this Clause 9, subject to the trigger events described herein.

9.2 The economic consideration shall consist of:

(a) a right to acquire or be allocated an equity interest in the Project Platform or its holding entity, as further described in Clause 10;

(b) a success-based cash fee payable upon achievement of Financial Close, as further described in Clause 11; and

(c) a development advance payable during the Development Period in accordance with Clause 12.

9.3 The Parties acknowledge that the economic entitlements set out in this Agreement reflect compensation for development risk, origination effort, and financing preparation, and are not intended to constitute remuneration for long-term operational management or employment.

---

## Clause 9A — Milestone Schedule

9A.1 A development milestone schedule shall be agreed by the Parties and annexed to this Agreement as Annexure B (Milestone Schedule), including feasibility completion, financing roadmap, lender engagement, and investment memorandum delivery.

9A.2 The milestone schedule may be updated by mutual written agreement of the Parties as the development pathway progresses.

---

## Clause 10 — Promoter Equity Entitlement

10.1 Subject to achievement of the applicable trigger events, the Development Promoter shall be entitled to an equity interest in the Project Platform or its designated holding entity (the "Promoter Equity").

10.2 The Promoter Equity shall represent the percentage specified in Annexure A (Promoter Equity Percentage) of the development-stage equity of the Project Platform (being the equity held by parties who assumed development risk prior to Financial Close), unless otherwise agreed in writing by the Parties.

10.3 The Promoter Equity shall vest exclusively upon:

(a) achievement of Financial Close; or

(b) execution of binding financing or contract funding documentation which, in substance, achieves project bankability and enables implementation.

10.4 No equity shall vest prior to the occurrence of the applicable trigger event, and no equity shall be deemed earned solely by reason of time elapsed or development effort undertaken.

10.5 Where the Origination Route is Route B and the Development Promoter has contributed Pre-existing Promoter IP or Pre-existing Promoter Relationships (as listed in Annexure A), such contribution shall be recognised in the determination of the Promoter Equity Percentage and shall be protected against dilution separately from development effort-based equity.

10.6 The detailed terms governing the issuance, holding, dilution, and governance rights associated with the Promoter Equity shall be documented in a shareholders' agreement or equivalent corporate documentation to be executed following Financial Close.

---

## Clause 11 — Success Fee

11.1 Upon achievement of Financial Close, the Launch Entity shall pay to the Development Promoter a success-based fee (the "Success Fee").

11.2 The Success Fee shall be calculated in accordance with the basis specified in Annexure A (Success Fee Basis) and the amount specified in Annexure A (Success Fee Amount).

11.3 The Success Fee shall become due and payable upon the earlier of:

(a) first drawdown of project financing; or

(b) receipt of committed funds under binding financing agreements.

11.4 The Parties may agree that all or part of the Success Fee shall be:

(a) offset against any Development Advance paid; or

(b) deferred and paid in instalments, where required by financing or cash flow constraints.

---

## Clause 12 — Development Advance and Catalytic Funding

12.1 During the Development Period, the Development Promoter shall be entitled to receive a monthly development advance in the amount specified in Annexure A (Monthly Advance Amount) (the "Development Advance"), for the initial term specified in Annexure A (Advance Term), subject to the conditions set out in this Clause. The Advance Term may be renewed by mutual written agreement for successive periods, subject to review of project progress and milestone achievement.

12.2 The Parties acknowledge and agree that the Development Advance constitutes:

(a) an advance against the Success Fee; and/or

(b) a reimbursable development cost payable from Catalytic Funding,

and does not constitute salary, employment remuneration, or operational management compensation.

12.3 The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to apply for Catalytic Funding intended to finance development activities, including feasibility studies, structuring work, and promoter development time.

12.4 Where Catalytic Funding is awarded and permits such use, Development Advances shall be paid from such funding sources.

12.5 In the event of timing mismatch between development activities and receipt of Catalytic Funding, the Parties may agree on interim bridging arrangements, subject always to reimbursement from Catalytic Funding where permissible.

---

## Clause 12A — Development Advance Risk Allocation

12A.1 Where the Origination Route is **Route A**, the Launch Entity shall ensure a minimum guaranteed monthly development advance as specified in Annexure A, recognising that the Development Promoter was sourced for the role and has assumed development risk at the invitation of the Launch Entity. The commencement and conditions of such guarantee are specified in Annexure A (Advance Commencement Trigger).

12A.2 Where the Origination Route is **Route B**, the Development Advance shall be payable subject to availability of Catalytic Funding and achievement of agreed milestones as set out in Annexure B (Milestone Schedule), recognising that the Development Promoter approached the development opportunity independently.

12A.3 Where Catalytic Funding is delayed or not obtained, the treatment of the Development Advance shall be as specified in Annexure A (Advance Shortfall Treatment).

---

## Clause 13 — Trigger Events and Determination

13.1 For the purposes of this Agreement, the following shall constitute recognised Trigger Events:

(a) award of Catalytic Funding;

(b) execution of binding financing documentation;

(c) achievement of Financial Close; or

(d) execution of alternative contract funding arrangements that, in substance, enable implementation of the Project.

13.2 The Parties agree that determination of whether a Trigger Event has occurred shall be based on objective documentary evidence, including executed agreements, funding award letters, or lender commitment documentation.

13.3 No Trigger Event shall be deemed to have occurred solely on the basis of non-binding expressions of interest, letters of support, or indicative term sheets.

13.4 The Parties shall act reasonably and in good faith in confirming the occurrence of any Trigger Event.

---

## Clause 14 — Transition Following Financial Close

14.1 Upon achievement of Financial Close, the Development Period shall automatically conclude, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

14.2 Following Financial Close:

(a) professional executive management may be appointed to operate the Project Platform;

(b) ownership and governance arrangements shall be formalised through shareholders' agreements or equivalent instruments;

(c) the Development Promoter's mandate under this Agreement shall terminate, except in respect of accrued rights.

14.3 The Parties acknowledge that the Development Promoter may, by mutual agreement:

(a) assume an executive role;

(b) hold a board or advisory position; or

(c) have no ongoing operational involvement,

without prejudice to its equity or economic entitlements earned under this Agreement.

14.4 The potential post-close roles contemplated are set out in Annexure A (Post-Close Role Variables). Nothing in this Agreement obliges the Development Promoter to assume any post-Financial Close role.

---

## Clause 15 — Intellectual Property and Development Outputs

15.1 All work product, documentation, studies, models, and development materials produced specifically for the Project during the Development Period (the "Development Outputs") shall be owned by the Project Platform, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

15.2 Pre-existing intellectual property of the Development Promoter, including methodologies, tools, templates, and know-how developed prior to this Agreement, shall remain the property of the Development Promoter.

15.3 Where the Origination Route is Route B and the Development Promoter has contributed Pre-existing Promoter IP (as listed in Annexure A), such intellectual property shall be:

(a) clearly identified and documented at the commencement of the Development Period;

(b) recognised as a distinct contribution separate from development effort; and

(c) subject to licensing or transfer terms to be agreed in the shareholders' agreement or equivalent documentation following Financial Close.

15.4 The Development Promoter grants to the Project Platform a non-exclusive, royalty-free licence to use pre-existing intellectual property contributed under this Agreement solely for purposes of developing, financing, and operating the Project.

15.5 Nothing in this Agreement shall transfer ownership of proprietary technology belonging to third-party vendors or consortium partners.

---

## Clause 16 — Confidentiality and Public Communications

16.1 Each Party shall keep confidential all non-public information relating to the Project, the other Party, and any consortium member, except where disclosure is required by law, funding authorities, or regulatory bodies.

16.2 Confidential information may be disclosed to advisers, financiers, grant authorities, and consortium partners strictly on a need-to-know basis.

16.3 No public announcement or external communication concerning the Project shall be made without prior written consent of the other Party, such consent not to be unreasonably withheld where disclosure is required for funding or regulatory purposes.

16.4 This Clause shall survive termination of this Agreement.

---

## Clause 17 — Exclusivity

17.1 During the Development Period, the Development Promoter shall act exclusively with respect to the Project and shall not promote or develop a directly competing infrastructure platform within the territory specified in Annexure A (Exclusivity Scope) without prior written consent of the Launch Entity.

17.2 Where the Origination Route is **Route B**, the Launch Entity shall likewise not engage an alternative promoter or developer for the same Project scope without prior written consent of the Development Promoter during the Development Period.

17.3 Exclusivity under this Clause is strictly limited to the Project and shall not restrict either Party from engaging in unrelated projects outside the scope specified in Annexure A.

17.4 The scope and duration of exclusivity are set out in Annexure A (Exclusivity Variables).

---

## Clause 18 — Representations, Warranties, and Limitation of Liability

18.1 Each Party represents and warrants that it is duly incorporated, validly existing, and authorized to enter into this Agreement.

18.2 The Development Promoter represents that it has relevant experience in infrastructure origination, development, and financing preparation.

18.3 Except as expressly stated herein, no Party makes any representation or warranty regarding the ultimate success, financing, or implementation of the Project.

18.4 To the maximum extent permitted by law, neither Party shall be liable to the other for indirect or consequential losses arising out of this Agreement.

---

## Clause 19 — Termination

19.1 This Agreement may be terminated:

(a) by mutual written agreement of the Parties;

(b) automatically upon achievement of Financial Close, except for clauses intended to survive;

(c) by either Party upon material breach by the other Party, provided such breach is not remedied within thirty (30) days of written notice; or

(d) where continued development becomes commercially impracticable due to regulatory, funding, or structural constraints.

19.2 Termination shall not affect:

(a) rights accrued prior to termination;

(b) confidentiality obligations;

(c) entitlement to Success Fees or equity already vested pursuant to Trigger Events.

19.3 In the event termination occurs prior to Financial Close, no Promoter Equity shall vest, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

---

## Clause 20 — Abandonment or Suspension by Launch Entity

20.1 If the Launch Entity elects to suspend or abandon the Project without material breach by the Development Promoter, the Development Promoter shall be entitled to:

(a) reimbursement of documented development costs; and

(b) any accrued Success Fee proportionate to progress achieved, as determined by reference to the Milestone Schedule (Annexure B).

---

## Clause 21 — Compliance and Anti-Corruption

21.1 Each Party represents that it shall comply with all applicable anti-corruption, anti-money laundering, and sanctions laws.

21.2 Breach of this Clause shall constitute material breach entitling the non-breaching Party to terminate this Agreement immediately.

---

## Clause 22 — Anti-Dilution Protection

22.1 Promoter Equity shall be calculated as a percentage of development-stage equity as at Financial Close, as specified in Annexure A.

22.2 No dilution shall occur prior to vesting.

22.3 Upon entry of investor equity (including DFI equity, strategic investors, or ECA-linked equity partners) at or after Financial Close, all development equity holders shall dilute pro rata. No development equity holder shall receive preferential anti-dilution protection unless unanimously agreed by all development equity holders.

22.4 The dilution treatment applicable to the Promoter Equity is set out in Annexure A (Dilution Treatment).

---

## Clause 23 — Change of Control Protection

23.1 Any change of control of the Launch Entity, Project SPV, or landholding entity shall not prejudice the Development Promoter's accrued or contingent rights.

23.2 All Success Fee and Promoter Equity entitlements shall survive and bind successors, transferees, and assigns.

---

## Clause 24 — Non-Circumvention and Protection of Economic Entitlements

24.1 The Launch Entity shall not, directly or indirectly, circumvent, bypass, or exclude the Development Promoter from the Project in order to avoid payment of the Success Fee or allocation of Promoter Equity.

24.2 If Financial Close occurs within twenty-four (24) months following termination using financiers, documentation, consortium members, or structuring introduced by the Development Promoter, the Development Promoter shall remain entitled to its Success Fee and Promoter Equity.

24.3 Where the Origination Route is **Route B**, the Development Promoter shall likewise not circumvent the Launch Entity or NexusNovus by directly approaching financiers, DFIs, ECAs, or consortium members introduced through the NexusNovus Incubation Framework, in order to develop the Project outside this framework. The twenty-four (24) month tail period in Clause 24.2 shall apply mutatis mutandis.

---

## Clause 25 — Project Governance — Steering Committee

25.1 A Development Steering Committee shall be established comprising equal representatives of each Party.

25.2 The Steering Committee shall approve major structural decisions, financing pathways, and consortium appointments.

25.3 Decisions shall require unanimous approval unless otherwise agreed.

25.4 In the event of deadlock, the Parties shall refer the matter to their respective senior executives for resolution within fourteen (14) days. Failing resolution, the matter may be escalated to the dispute resolution process in Clause 29.

---

## Clause 26 — Independent Contractor Relationship

26.1 The Development Promoter acts as an independent contractor and not as an employee, partner, or agent of the Launch Entity.

26.2 Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating:

(a) an employment relationship;

(b) a partnership or joint venture; or

(c) authority for either Party to bind the other.

26.3 The internal employment or contractual arrangements between the Development Promoter and its personnel are solely its responsibility.

---

## Clause 27 — Novation to Project SPV

27.1 Upon incorporation of the Project SPV, this Agreement shall be novated to such SPV.

27.2 All rights of the Development Promoter, including Success Fee and Promoter Equity, shall survive such novation.

---

## Clause 28 — Assignment Restrictions

28.1 No Party may assign its rights under this Agreement without prior written consent of the other Party, except to an affiliate or SPV, subject to all promoter protections under this Agreement being preserved.

---

## Clause 29 — Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

29.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws specified in Annexure A (Governing Law).

29.2 Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be resolved through good-faith negotiations between senior representatives of the Parties.

29.3 Failing resolution within thirty (30) days, disputes shall be finally resolved by arbitration in the venue and in accordance with the rules specified in Annexure A (Dispute Resolution).

---

## Clause 30 — Good Faith and Cooperation

30.1 Each Party shall act in good faith and not unreasonably withhold approvals, information, or cooperation required for financing progression.

---

## Clause 31 — Costs and Expenses

31.1 Each Party bears its own costs unless otherwise agreed.

31.2 Third-party feasibility costs shall be pre-approved in writing.

---

## Clause 32 — Survival

32.1 Clauses relating to Non-Circumvention (Clause 24), Economic Entitlements (Clauses 9–12A), Confidentiality (Clause 16), Intellectual Property (Clause 15), Dispute Resolution (Clause 29), and Compliance (Clause 21) shall survive termination of this Agreement.

---

## Clause 33 — Miscellaneous

33.1 This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties in relation to its subject matter.

33.2 Any amendment must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

33.3 Neither Party may assign its rights under this Agreement without prior written consent, except to an affiliate.

33.4 This Agreement may be executed in counterparts.
