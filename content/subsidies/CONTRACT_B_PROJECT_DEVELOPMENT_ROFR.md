## 1. Project Context

1.1 The Promoter is originating a project requiring multiple technology components to achieve eligibility for institutional and frontier funding.

1.2 The Project consists of several Technology Classes, each of which may be supplied by multiple potential providers.

1.3 The Exporter/Vendor has been identified as a best-in-class provider within its Technology Class.

1.4 The Orchestrator coordinates project structuring and development activities aimed at achieving funding eligibility.

---

## 2. Nature of Participation

2.1 Participation under this Agreement is non-exclusive and evaluative in nature.

2.2 No assurance is given that the Project will proceed or that any technology will be selected.

2.3 This Agreement does not constitute a procurement or supply agreement.

---

## 3. Role Clarification of NexusNovus

3.1 NexusNovus Capital B.V. acts solely as project coordinator and development orchestrator.

3.2 Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating a fiduciary, trustee, partnership, agency, investment advisory or joint venture relationship. NexusNovus shall not be deemed a promoter, sponsor, investor or supplier under the Project.

---

## 4. Sub-Scope of Work

4.1 The Exporter/Vendor shall perform the sub-scope described in Annex 1 (the "**Sub-Scope**").

4.2 The Sub-Scope is intended to demonstrate technical feasibility and integration suitability.

4.3 Deliverables shall be provided in good faith and with reasonable professional skill.

---

## 5. Business Development Budget

5.1 The Exporter/Vendor agrees to allocate a business development budget as consideration for the rights granted herein.

5.2 The budget and payment mechanics are set out in Annex A (Development Budget Note).

5.3 Amounts payable under Annex A are payable to NexusNovus Capital B.V., which shall disburse funds on behalf of the Parties.

---

## 6. Budget Adjustment and Reallocation

6.1 Where an updated certificate or budget confirmation reflects a lower available amount, the Orchestrator may reallocate the budget within the approved scope.

6.2 Reallocation shall be performed acting reasonably and in good faith.

6.3 The Development Budget Note may include budget amounts allocated back to the Exporter/Vendor for work performed.

---

## 7. Budget Administration and No Escrow Relationship

Any Development Budget paid to NexusNovus is received as contractual consideration and for administrative disbursement purposes only. No trust, escrow or fiduciary relationship is created. NexusNovus shall disburse funds in accordance with documented scope approvals and shall have no obligation to pre-fund or advance monies.

---

## 8. Non-Refundable Nature of Development Budget

The Development Budget constitutes non-refundable commercial consideration for project coordination, structuring efforts and the grant of the Right of First Refusal (ROFR), except in cases where NexusNovus expressly waives it off.

---

## 9. Right of First Refusal

9.1 In consideration of the Business Development Budget, the Promoter grants the Exporter/Vendor a conditional right of first refusal within its Technology Class.

9.2 The ROFR applies only if the Project proceeds to selection.

9.3 The ROFR permits matching of competing offers within a commercially reasonable bandwidth.

---

## 10. ROFR Procedure

10.1 The Promoter shall notify the Exporter/Vendor of principal commercial terms.

10.2 The Exporter/Vendor shall have twenty (20) Business Days to exercise the ROFR.

10.3 Failure to respond constitutes waiver for that selection event.

---

## 11. ROFR Clarification and Limitations

11.1 The ROFR shall apply solely within the defined Technology Class and shall remain valid for a period of three (3) years from the Effective Date.

11.2 Matching rights require economic equivalence to competing bona fide offers.

11.3 The ROFR shall not apply to equity investments, strategic acquisitions or corporate-level transactions.

---

## 12. Intellectual Property

12.1 Work product generated under the Sub-Scope shall vest in the Promoter.

12.2 Pre-existing IP remains with the Exporter/Vendor.

12.3 A royalty-free licence is granted for evaluation and funding purposes.

---

## 13. Intellectual Property Clarification

13.1 Background intellectual property remains the sole property of the originating Party. Foreground IP shall transfer only upon full payment of applicable amounts. Prior to such payment, only a limited, non-exclusive licence for evaluation purposes is granted.

13.2 The Exporter/Vendor shall indemnify the other Parties for IP infringement claims.

---

## 14. Representations and Warranties

14.1 Each Party represents and warrants that:

(a) it is duly incorporated and validly existing;

(b) it has authority to enter into this Agreement;

(c) execution does not violate any law or existing contractual obligation;

(d) all information provided for project development is accurate and not misleading.

---

## 15. Non-Circumvention

The Parties shall not circumvent or bypass NexusNovus in respect of funding structures, institutional introductions or derivative projects originating from this Project for a period of three (3) years.

---

## 16. Irrevocability

16.1 From commencement of Sub-Scope performance or incurrence of material cost, the Exporter/Vendor's obligations become irrevocable.

16.2 Termination thereafter does not release payment obligations.

---

## 17. Indemnity in Favour of NexusNovus

17.1 The Exporter/Vendor and Promoter shall jointly and severally indemnify and hold harmless NexusNovus from any losses, claims, liabilities, damages, penalties, regulatory actions, IP infringement claims, or third-party disputes arising from:

(a) inaccurate information;

(b) breach of funding rules;

(c) IP violations;

(d) project implementation failures not attributable to NexusNovus.

18.1 The aggregate liability of NexusNovus shall be limited to 20% of the Development Budget actually received by it.

18.2 NexusNovus shall not be liable for indirect, consequential or loss of profit damages, nor for failure of funding, non-selection or project cancellation.

---

## 19. Change in Law and Funding Rules

NexusNovus shall not be liable for changes in law, EU taxonomy rules, funding criteria or institutional policy that affect project eligibility or selection.

---

## 20. Compliance with Laws and Sanctions

Each Party shall comply with applicable Dutch, EU and international laws including anti-bribery, anti-money laundering and sanctions regulations. Any breach entitles NexusNovus to immediate termination.

---

## 21. Termination

21.1 Either Party may terminate for material breach following twenty-five (25) Business Days' notice.

21.2 Termination does not affect accrued rights or payment obligations.

---

## 22. Insolvency and SPV Risk

22.1 Immediate termination rights apply upon insolvency, suspension of payments or dissolution of a Party.

22.2 Where the Promoter is an SPV, NexusNovus may request additional financial assurances or guarantees.

---

## 23. Confidentiality

All technical data, financial models, funding structures, and commercial information shared under this Agreement shall remain confidential for five (5) years following termination.

---

## 24. Miscellaneous

24.2 Amendments must be in writing.

24.3 Dutch law governs this Agreement and disputes shall be submitted to the courts of Amsterdam.

---

Prior to initiating court proceedings, the Parties shall attempt good faith negotiation for thirty (30) days. Failing resolution, disputes shall be submitted to the courts of Amsterdam.
